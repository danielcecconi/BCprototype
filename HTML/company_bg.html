Company Background:

Starting as a small collective in Hahndorf, South Australia, a township known for its lively arts and crafts community, Bazaar Ceramics has been operating for over 20 years. In its first seven years of existence Bazaar Ceramics operated as a co-operative. Memebrs were responsible for designing, throwing, glazing and firing their own work.

As the studio's reputation grew, Bazaar Ceramics transitioned into its current form. Emma Rich purchased the business and moved it into a larger premises located in Stepney, Adelaide. The staff burgeoned, with 2 full time ceramic designers as well as six production potters being added to the Bazaar Ceramics production team.

Bazaar Ceramics currently produces a wide range of products sought after by both national and international collectors alike, with approximately 40% of all work being done on a for comission basis. Pieces can be found in hotels, board rooms as well as privite homes both locally and as far away as the U.S. and Germany. A company on the gorw, Bazaar Ceramics is truly exapnding to become one of Australia's leading Ceramics manufacturer.
